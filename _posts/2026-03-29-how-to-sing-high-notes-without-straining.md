High notes are the number one challenge I hear from singers. "I want to sing higher without cracking." "I can hit the note but it sounds strained."

Here's the thing: high notes aren't harder than low notes. They just need different coordination.

## The Strain Problem

When you strain for a high note, you're squeezing your throat. You're fighting your voice instead of working with it.

Think about reaching for something on a high shelf. Do you tense your neck and stretch your chin up? No — you reach with your arm, relaxed. Singing works the same way.

## Register Basics

Your voice has different registers:

- **Chest voice** — Lower, fuller sound. Vocal cords are thick.
- **Head voice** — Higher, lighter sound. Vocal cords stretch and thin.

The "crack" happens when you switch abruptly between them. The goal isn't eliminating the switch — it's making it smooth.

## The Lip Trill (Your New Best Friend)

If I could give you one exercise for high notes, this is it.

1. Relax your lips
2. Blow air through them so they flap (like a horse)
3. Add voice: "brrrrrrrr"
4. Slide from low to high like a siren

Why it works:
- Keeps breath flowing (can't trill without air)
- Prevents throat tension (try straining while trilling — you can't)
- Connects registers automatically

Do 5 sirens daily. Your comfortable high note will get higher.

## The "NG" Sound

Say "sing" and hold the "ng" at the end. Feel the buzz in your nose and face.

Slide from low to high on "ng." Keep the buzz consistent.

This finds your head voice without pushing. Once you can slide smoothly, add words.

## Vowel Tweaks

Some vowels fight you on high notes:

- **"EE"** (as in "see") tends to squeeze → think "ih" (as in "sit") instead
- **"AH"** on high notes → think "aw" (as in "saw") for more space

Small adjustments make a big difference.

## The "Cry" Approach

Think of how your voice sounds when you're genuinely emotional — about to cry, expressing feeling. That slightly held quality? That's the position for easy high notes.

Not about sounding whiny. About finding the physical coordination that makes high notes easy.

## Daily Practice

10 minutes:
1. Lip trill sirens (3 min)
2. NG slides (3 min)
3. Vowel scales on comfortable high notes (4 min)

Stop if it hurts. Discomfort means something's wrong.
