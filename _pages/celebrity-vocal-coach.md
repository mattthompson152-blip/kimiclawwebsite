    <h2>The "Easy Way" Method</h2>
    
    <p>I call my approach the "Easy Way" because I break down complex vocal techniques into simple, practical steps. Instead of confusing jargon, I give you concrete things to feel and do.</p>
    
    <div style="background: #1a1a1a; border-radius: 8px; padding: 2rem; margin: 2rem 0; border: 1px solid #333;">
      <p style="margin-bottom: 1rem;"><strong style="color: #737373;">Instead of:</strong> "Place the voice in the mask with proper resonance"</p>
      <p style="margin-bottom: 0; color: #ff6b4a;"><strong>I say:</strong> "Hum and feel the buzz in your lips. That's where your voice lives."</p>
    </div>
    
    <div style="background: #1a1a1a; border-radius: 8px; padding: 2rem; margin: 2rem 0; border: 1px solid #333;">
      <p style="margin-bottom: 1rem;"><strong style="color: #737373;">Instead of:</strong> "Support with the diaphragm while maintaining a lifted soft palate"</p>
      <p style="margin-bottom: 0; color: #ff6b4a;"><strong>I say:</strong> "Breathe low like you're smelling a flower. Keep that openness when you sing."</p>
    </div>
