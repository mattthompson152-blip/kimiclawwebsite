      <h2>Getting Started</h2>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>What happens in the first lesson?</h3>
        <p>Your first lesson is a <strong>vocal assessment and goal-setting session</strong>. We'll discuss your singing history and what you want to achieve, assess your current vocal strengths and areas for improvement, work through some exercises to understand your voice, create a personalized plan for your development, and answer any questions you have about the process.</p>
        <p>Most students leave their first lesson with specific exercises to practice and a clear understanding of how to move forward.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>Do I need any special equipment for online lessons?</h3>
        <p><strong>Absolutely not.</strong> If you have a smartphone, tablet, or computer with a camera and internet connection, you're ready to go.</p>
        
        <p><strong>Recommended but not required:</strong></p>
        <ul>
          <li>Headphones or earbuds (reduces echo and improves sound)</li>
          <li>A quiet space where you feel comfortable singing</li>
          <li>Good lighting so I can see your posture and facial expressions</li>
          <li>A second device for playing backing tracks (optional but helpful)</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p><strong>What you DON'T need:</strong> Expensive microphones, professional recording equipment, or a piano.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>What platforms do you use for online lessons?</h3>
        <p>I teach via <strong>FaceTime, WhatsApp, or Microsoft Teams</strong> — whatever works best for you.</p>
        <p>When you book a lesson, you'll automatically receive a Microsoft Teams appointment with a link. You can join directly through your web browser — no account or app download required.</p>
        <p>If you prefer FaceTime or WhatsApp, just let me know when you book.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>How quickly will I see improvement?</h3>
        <p><strong>Most students notice changes immediately</strong> in their first lesson — a higher note they couldn't hit before, less strain, or clearer tone.</p>
        
        <p><strong>Sustainable improvement</strong> typically shows within:</p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>2-4 weeks:</strong> Better vocal control and awareness</li>
          <li><strong>1-3 months:</strong> Significant range expansion and confidence</li>
          <li><strong>3-6 months:</strong> Transformed vocal ability and performance readiness</li>
        </ul>
        
        <p>The key is <strong>consistent practice</strong> between lessons. Students who practice 15-20 minutes daily see results faster than those who only sing during our sessions.</p>
      </div>

      <h2>About the Lessons</h2>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>What styles do you teach?</h3>
        <p>I teach <strong>all contemporary styles</strong>, including Pop, Rock, Musical Theatre, Jazz, Blues, Soul, R&B, Funk, and Gospel.</p>
        <p>My "Easy Way" method works across all genres because it focuses on the fundamentals of healthy vocal production — breath support, register blending, and tension-free technique.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>Do you teach beginners?</h3>
        <p><strong>Absolutely.</strong> About half of my students are complete beginners. I've developed specific approaches for absolute beginners who've never sung in front of anyone, returning singers who haven't sung in years, hobby singers who want to improve for personal enjoyment, and career singers preparing for professional work.</p>
        <p>No matter your starting point, I'll meet you where you are.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>How are the lessons structured?</h3>
        <p>A typical 60-minute lesson includes:</p>
        
        <ol>
          <li><strong>Check-in</strong> (5 min): How was your practice? Any challenges?</li>
          <li><strong>Warm-up</strong> (10-15 min): Tailored exercises for your voice that day</li>
          <li><strong>Technique work</strong> (15-20 min): Specific exercises addressing your goals</li>
          <li><strong>Repertoire/Application</strong> (15-20 min): Applying technique to songs</li>
          <li><strong>Practice plan</strong> (5 min): What to work on before next lesson</li>
        </ol>
        
        <p>Every lesson is <strong>recorded</strong> (with your permission) so you can review it later. You'll also receive written practice notes after each session.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>Can you help with audition preparation?</h3>
        <p><strong>Yes — this is one of my specialties.</strong> I've prepared singers for West End and Broadway auditions, TV talent shows (X Factor, The Voice, Britain's Got Talent), drama school entry auditions, cruise ship and resort contracts, and recording sessions.</p>
        <p>We'll work on choosing the right material for your voice and the role, technical preparation for challenging passages, performance coaching (acting through song), managing nerves and building confidence, and last-minute troubleshooting.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="faq-item">
        <h3>What if I'm tone deaf?</h3>
        <p><strong>You're probably not.</strong> True tone deafness (amusia) affects less than 4% of the population. Most people who think they can't sing have simply never been shown how.</p>
        <p>I've taught hundreds of "non-singers" who now perform confidently. If you can speak, you can learn to sing.</p>
      </div>
