      <h2>Common Beginner Concerns</h2>
      
      <h3>"I'm Tone Deaf"</h3>
      <p>You're probably not. True tone deafness is rare. Most people who think they can't match pitch simply haven't learned how — yet. With the right exercises, pitch matching is a skill anyone can develop.</p>
      
      <h3>"I'm Too Old to Start"</h3>
      <p>My beginner students range from teenagers to people in their 60s and 70s. It's never too late. In fact, adult beginners often progress faster because they're motivated and practice consistently.</p>
      
      <h3>"I'll Be Embarrassed"</h3>
      <p>The fear is real, but unnecessary. I've worked with beginners who literally couldn't make a sound on their first lesson. Within weeks, they were singing confidently. The only thing to be embarrassed about is never trying.</p>
      
      <h3>"I Don't Have Time to Practice"</h3>
      <p>15 minutes a day is enough. Sing in the shower. Hum while driving. Do exercises while making tea. Small, consistent practice beats occasional long sessions.</p>
