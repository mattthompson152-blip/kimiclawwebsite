      <h2>Why Virtual Singing Lessons Work</h2>
      
      <p>When I first started teaching online, I was skeptical. Could virtual singing lessons really match in-person training? <strong>The answer surprised me: often, they're better.</strong></p>
      
      <h3>The Comfort Factor</h3>
      <p>Students sing better when they're comfortable. In a virtual lesson, you're in your own space — your living room, your bedroom, wherever you feel most at ease. No intimidating studio rooms, no travel stress, no waiting areas. Just you and your voice.</p>
      
      <h3>Better Focus on Technique</h3>
      <p>Without the visual distractions of a physical space, students often focus more intently on their own technique. You're looking at a screen, but you're listening to your body — and that's exactly where your attention should be.</p>
      
      <h3>Recorded for Review</h3>
      <p>Every virtual lesson can be recorded (with your permission), giving you a permanent reference. Review the exercises, hear your progress, practice with the recording — something that's rarely practical in traditional lessons.</p>

      <h2>What You Need for Virtual Singing Lessons</h2>
      
      <h3>Required (You Definitely Have This)</h3>
      
      <ul>
        <li>A smartphone, tablet, or computer with a camera</li>
        <li>Internet connection</li>
        <li>A space where you can sing comfortably</li>
      </ul>
      
      <h3>Recommended (Helpful But Optional)</h3>
      
      <ul>
        <li>Headphones or earbuds (reduces echo, improves sound)</li>
        <li>Good lighting so I can see your posture</li>
        <li>Second device for playing backing tracks (optional)</li>
      </ul>
      
      <p><strong>That's it.</strong> No expensive microphones, no professional recording equipment, no piano. The simplicity is the point.</p>

      <h2>What Happens in a Virtual Singing Lesson</h2>
      
      <p><strong>The same personalized coaching you'd get in person:</strong></p>
      
      <ol>
        <li><strong>Check-in</strong> (5 min): How did your practice go? Any challenges since last time?</li>
        <li><strong>Warm-up</strong> (10-15 min): Tailored exercises specific to your voice that day</li>
        <li><strong>Technique work</strong> (15-20 min): Specific exercises addressing your goals — range extension, breath support, tension release, etc.</li>
        <li><strong>Repertoire</strong> (15-20 min): Applying technique to songs you want to sing</li>
        <li><strong>Practice plan</strong> (5 min): Clear instructions for what to work on before next lesson</li>
      </ol>
      
      <p>Plus: <strong>recorded lesson and written practice notes</strong> after every session.</p>
